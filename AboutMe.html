Hi there! 👋

My name is Jan, and I’m currently a second-year student of Information Technology at the Faculty of Education, Charles University. I’ve been interested in computers and technology since I was a kid — I’ve always been fascinated by how things work, what can be changed or improved, and why things look the way they do. 💻

Gaming 🎮 has been a big part of my life from early on. For me, games are more than just entertainment — they’re a way to relax, unwind, and escape from reality for a while. Thanks to gaming, I’ve learned a lot of English 🇬🇧, and with games like Path of Exile, I really enjoy the strategy and thinking behind them. Sometimes, there’s just nothing better than turning everything off, opening a game, and getting lost in another world. 😄

Since elementary school, I’ve been playing tennis 🎾 — my parents and grandparents guided me toward competitive play, so I trained seriously and gradually went through all the categories from young players up to adults. For a while, I even played for two towns on weekends – Zruč nad Sázavou (youth) and Ledeč nad Sázavou (adults). In high school, I was probably in the best shape of my life — my friend and I trained five to seven times a week.

After graduation, I went to a Higher Vocational School focused on Business Economics and Management. I originally wanted to study IT, but I didn’t get in at the time, so I chose this path instead — and it turned out to be a valuable experience. During my internship, I worked with CZECH.UP z.s., an organization focused on gamified education, where we created educational online courses on topics like cybersecurity and financial literacy.

In my internship evaluation, it was noted that I was considered a co-author of several projects, and I also earned a certificate as Lead Methodologist of the CZECH.UP JukeBox Platform for Gamified Learning. During this time, I led a team of students and friends in a project about financial literacy in cooperation with ČPP, and I also took part in international collaboration where CZECH.UP (CZ) worked together with partners from Slovakia 🇸🇰, Italy 🇮🇹, and Spain 🇪🇸. This experience taught me communication, leadership, and how to work effectively online. 🌍

After successfully completing my studies, I passed my final exams in Economics, Accounting, Management, Costing, and English, and earned the DiS. title (Certified Specialist) — with honors! 🎓 Of course, my friends and I — the same group who studied together for three years — had to celebrate properly 🍻, and even though we don’t see each other as often now, we still keep in touch.

Shortly after returning from a family vacation, I received an email that completely changed my plans — I was accepted to Charles University to study Information Technology with a focus on education, a field I had wanted to pursue for a long time. And it was there that I discovered what truly excites me — web development. 🖥️

In my first year, I enrolled in an optional course called Web Development, where I learned the basics of HTML and CSS and built my first website — Custom Project Company, which you can find in the Projects & News section. That’s where it all began.

Now, in my second year, I’m taking the course again — but this time as a mandatory subject with a broader scope. Since all of us have already built websites before, we go through HTML and CSS faster, so we can spend more time on JavaScript, databases, and MySQL.

I really enjoy working with websites — from designing layouts and tuning colors to writing the code itself. I like when everything fits together naturally and the result looks clean and balanced. ✨ I always meet deadlines, I’m reliable, and communication with me is easy — I usually reply within a few minutes. 😄

In my free time, I love to play games 🎮 — gaming has been a big part of my life for years and still is. Sometimes I try out new things in HTML/CSS or do a bit of coding, but when I’m not creating, I like to watch Outdoor Boys, grab a beer 🍺, and just relax. I’m also learning Python and experimenting with the Pygame library, where I’m slowly learning to make simple games — it’s still more of an experiment, but I really enjoy it. 🐍

I’m truly grateful for the people around me ❤️ — for my mom, who always supports me and does everything she can to make sure I’m happy 🌷, for my grandparents, who help and give me valuable advice 👵👴, and for my girlfriend, who supports me, believes in me, and stands by me even when I doubt myself. 💞

If you like my work, projects, or my story, feel free to reach out. Maybe we can create something together that perfectly fits your vision. 🤝
